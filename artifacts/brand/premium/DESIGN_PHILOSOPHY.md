# Field Intelligence

Field Intelligence is a visual language for reading football as a living map. The pitch is not decoration; it is the coordinate system. Lines, circles, and zones behave like tactical annotations, pressure fields, and broadcast overlays, forming a disciplined identity that feels analytical without becoming cold. The work should look meticulously crafted, measured, and resolved, as if every point and line was placed after countless refinements by someone at the top of their field.

The palette is restrained and authoritative: night green as the base, pitch green as the field, signal gold as the active layer, and warm report paper as the human note. Color does not shout. It marks priority. Gold appears only where the brand is pointing to the signal: the underline, the ring, the active route, the moment of attention. This restraint creates trust and makes the identity feel like a premium sports intelligence desk rather than generic fan media.

The logo should behave like a badge, not a mascot. Its monogram is bold enough for a tiny social avatar, with all supporting geometry pushed into quiet hierarchy. The best version reads instantly at 40px and still rewards close inspection at full size. Space is a core material: the center is calm, the edges carry motion, and the composition uses asymmetry in background forms while keeping the main mark optically balanced. Nothing should feel accidental.

Typography is direct, compact, and editorial. It should feel like a match report meeting a control room: strong headlines, sparse labels, and no decorative filler. Text is used as a signal layer, not a paragraph layer. On social assets, the words must be few enough to be remembered and large enough to survive mobile compression.

Every asset should feel like part of a system: avatar, banner, post, thumbnail, and app view should all speak the same language. The final result must feel painstakingly finished: crisp exports, stable alignment, strong contrast, clean safe areas, and no visual clutter that weakens the mark.
